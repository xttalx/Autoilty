🔧 Complete Vehicle Maintenance Schedule

Keep your car running smoothly with proper preventive maintenance

📅 Maintenance Intervals Guide

Regular maintenance is the key to vehicle longevity and reliability. This comprehensive guide covers maintenance intervals for typical passenger vehicles in Canadian conditions. Always consult your owner's manual for manufacturer-specific requirements.

⚠️ Important: Canadian driving conditions are often classified as "severe" by manufacturers due to extreme temperatures, short trips, and salt exposure. Follow "severe service" schedules, which typically require more frequent maintenance.

Every 6 Months or 8,000km (Whichever Comes First):

🛢️ Oil & Filter Change

Most critical maintenance item. Synthetic oil can extend to 10,000km but check manufacturer specs. Cost: $60-120

🔍 Visual Inspection

Check fluid levels, belts, hoses, lights, wipers, tire condition. Cost: Usually free with oil change

🚗 Tire Rotation

Promotes even wear, extends tire life. Essential every other oil change. Cost: $30-50 (often free)

Every Year or 15,000km:

🌬️ Cabin Air Filter

Improves HVAC performance, air quality. DIY-friendly. Cost: $15-40 DIY, $50-80 shop

💨 Engine Air Filter

Affects fuel economy and performance. Easy DIY. Cost: $20-50 DIY, $40-80 shop

🔋 Battery Test

Load test annually, especially before winter. Check terminals for corrosion. Cost: Free at most shops

🛞 Tire Balance & Alignment Check

Balance prevents vibration, alignment saves tires. Cost: $80-150 combined

Every 2 Years or 30,000km:

🚿 Coolant Flush

Prevents overheating, protects engine. Critical in Canadian extremes. Cost: $100-150

🦴 Brake Fluid Flush

Absorbs moisture over time, reducing effectiveness. Often overlooked. Cost: $80-120

🔦 Bulb & Light Check

Replace all exterior bulbs, clean lenses. Cost: $20-100 DIY, varies by vehicle

Every 3-4 Years or 50,000km:

🛞 Tires

Replace when tread depth reaches 4/32" (legal minimum 2/32"). Check for age cracks. Cost: $600-1200 set

🔋 Battery Replacement

Lifespan 3-5 years in Canada. Don't wait for failure. Cost: $150-300

🛑 Brake Pads

Varies by driving style. Listen for squealing. Inspect during rotations. Cost: $200-400 per axle

⚡ Spark Plugs

Modern platinum/iridium plugs last 80-160k km. Affects fuel economy. Cost: $200-400

💰 Annual Maintenance Cost Estimates

New Car (0-3 years)

$500-800/year
Mainly oil changes, tire rotations, inspections

Mid-Age (4-7 years)

$1,000-1,500/year
Add brakes, tires, battery, fluids

Older (8+ years)

$1,500-2,500/year
More frequent repairs, worn components

💡 Money-Saving Tip: Buy maintenance packages when purchasing a new vehicle. Many dealerships offer pre-paid maintenance at significant discounts ($1,500-2,000 for 3 years of service typically worth $3,000+).

🛠️ DIY vs Professional Service

Easy DIY Maintenance (Save $$$):

  • ✓ Air filter replacement (5 minutes, save $30)
  • ✓ Cabin filter replacement (10 minutes, save $40)
  • ✓ Wiper blade replacement (2 minutes, save $20)
  • ✓ Light bulb replacement (varies, save $20-50)
  • ✓ Tire pressure check (weekly, free, extends tire life)

Leave to Professionals:

  • ✗ Brake work (safety-critical)
  • ✗ Suspension repairs (alignment needed)
  • ✗ Timing belt (engine damage if incorrect)
  • ✗ Transmission service (specialized tools)
  • ✗ Coolant flush (proper disposal required)
