🛞 Complete Tire Buying Guide

Everything you need to know about choosing the right tires for Canadian conditions

📏 Understanding Tire Sizes

Your tire size is printed on the sidewall. Example: P215/65R15 95H

P = Passenger vehicle (LT = Light Truck)

215 = Width in millimeters

65 = Aspect ratio (sidewall height as % of width)

R = Radial construction (standard for modern tires)

15 = Rim diameter in inches

95 = Load index (capacity rating)

H = Speed rating (up to 210 km/h)

⚠️ Important: Always match the size specified in your owner's manual or driver's door jamb sticker. Wrong sizes affect speedometer accuracy, handling, and safety systems (ABS, traction control).

🔄 Tire Types Explained

❄️ Winter Tires

Use: Nov-Apr in Canada
Pros: Superior cold weather grip, mandated in Quebec
Cost: $600-1200/set

☀️ All-Season Tires

Use: Year-round (mild climates)
Pros: Convenience, decent all-around
Cost: $500-1000/set

🌦️ All-Weather Tires

Use: True 4-season (⛰️ symbol)
Pros: One set year-round, better than all-season in winter
Cost: $700-1300/set

🏎️ Performance/Summer

Use: Warm weather only
Pros: Maximum dry/wet grip
Cost: $800-2000/set

❄️ Winter vs All-Season: The Facts

Stopping Distance on Ice (30 km/h to 0):

  • 🛑 Winter Tires: 7.5 meters
  • 🛑 All-Season Tires: 12+ meters

That's 60% longer stopping distance! In Canadian winters, winter tires aren't optional - they're life-saving equipment.

💡 All-Weather Compromise: Nokian, Michelin CrossClimate 2, and Goodyear WeatherReady are true all-weather tires (3-peak mountain snowflake rated). Good option if you can't store/swap two sets, though dedicated winter tires still perform better in harsh conditions.

⭐ Top Tire Brands Ranked

Premium Tier ($$$):

1. Michelin: Best longevity (80-100k km), quietest, excellent all-around. Premium price justified.

2. Bridgestone: Blizzak series is winter gold standard. Strong performance across lineup.

3. Continental: European engineering, exceptional wet grip, great winter options.

Mid-Range ($$):

4. Goodyear: Solid all-around, good availability, competitive pricing.

5. Nokian: Finnish winter specialists. Best for extreme cold conditions.

6. Pirelli: Performance-oriented, good for sporty vehicles.

Budget Tier ($):

7. Hankook/Kumho: Korean brands, decent quality at lower price. Good budget choice.

8. General/Firestone: Budget options from major manufacturers. Acceptable for low-mileage drivers.

❌ Avoid: Unknown Chinese brands from discount retailers ($50/tire). False economy - wear fast, poor grip, safety risk. Tires are your only contact with the road. Don't cheap out.

🕒 When to Replace Tires

The Coin Test:

Insert a quarter into the tread groove:

  • ✓ Caribou nose hidden: Tread depth good (5/32" +)
  • ⚠️ Caribou nose visible: Consider replacement soon (4/32")
  • ❌ Top of caribou visible: Replace immediately (legal minimum 2/32")

Other Replacement Indicators:

  • 🗓️ Age: Replace after 6 years regardless of tread. Rubber degrades. Check DOT code on sidewall.
  • 🔍 Cracks: Sidewall cracking (dry rot) = immediate replacement
  • 🎈 Bulges: Indicates internal damage. Blowout risk.
  • 📊 Uneven Wear: Edge wear, center wear, cupping = alignment or pressure issues
  • 📳 Vibration: Constant vibration at highway speed = balance or internal damage

💰 Pro Tip: Don't wait until you're at minimum tread. Replace winter tires at 5/32" and all-season at 4/32" for optimal safety and performance. Worn tires in Canadian conditions are dangerous.

💵 Cost-Saving Strategies

Buy Off-Season

Purchase winter tires in spring/summer, all-seasons in winter. Save 15-30%.

Consider "Take-Offs"

New car owners often sell factory tires immediately. Get 95% new tires for 50% off.

Buy Sets of 4

Never replace just 2 tires. Uneven traction is dangerous, especially in winter.

Maintain Proper Pressure

Check monthly. Under-inflation = 25% shorter lifespan. Free air at most gas stations.

Rotate Regularly

Every 8-10k km. Even wear extends life by 20-30%.

Buy Rims for Winter Set

$400-800 upfront, save $80-120/year on swaps. Breaks even in 4-5 years.
